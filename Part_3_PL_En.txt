A chapter of "The acts of Einar the Peacock and The Golden Deer, a fellowship of his. By Wigon of Hamburg", A.D. 956 

On the very first day of the week that began after the Epiphany, a clamour happened among the guildmates of Einar's. After judging that the pagans must have begun their drunkenness and licentiousness again, I have left the hut, that was assigned to me by Einar, to rebuke marauders and show them the Lord's way.

To my bafflement, instead of sinful misdemeanours, I have witnessed crying men, whose faces, full of joy, have been announcing jubilant tidings.
As it has unfolded later, on this day Egil the huscarl, son of Udulf, returned to the home of Einar's after a lengthy absence. Clothed in a tunic, with a helmet of the head of his, covered in ancient symbols shield with and a proven sword, 
he entered the young jarl's lodging. On this day the pagans thanked their gods and celebrated their old kin's return who has helped to defeat a plague spreading in a faraway kingdom. 

In return he has joined friendly battles to, without mercy, but to the joy of idle spectators, put their bodies back to the ground using the sword of his.
Even I have pled in prayers the Highest to, with His presence, help Egil in the quest for our cause.
